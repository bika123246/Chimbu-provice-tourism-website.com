Welcome to the Land of the Unexpected

A journey to the roof of Papua New Guinea

Nestled in the dramatic central highlands, Chimbu (or Simbu) Province is a realm of breathtaking beauty and ancient cultures. Home to Mount Wilhelm, the nation's highest peak, Chimbu offers a raw, authentic experience for the intrepid traveler. From its colorful "singsing" festivals to its challenging mountain treks, this is a place where nature's grandeur and human resilience are on full display. Prepare to be captivated by the spirit of Chimbu.

What to See & Do

From majestic mountains to winding rivers, adventure awaits.

Climb Mount Wilhelm

Embark on the ultimate challenge: a trek to the 4,509-metre summit of Papua New Guinea's highest mountain. Witness stunning alpine scenery and unforgettable sunrises.

Experience a "Singsing"

Immerse yourself in the vibrant culture at a traditional gathering. Marvel at the intricate body paint, elaborate headdresses, and rhythmic dances of the Chimbu people.

Explore the Waghi Valley

Discover the lush, fertile Waghi Valley, known for its coffee and tea plantations. Enjoy the scenic drives and learn about the local agriculture that sustains the region.

The Heartbeat of the Highlands

Discover a culture woven from ancient stories and traditions.

A Rich Tapestry of Life

The people of Chimbu are renowned for their resilience and deep connection to their land. Their culture is expressed through powerful oral histories, intricate craftsmanship, and the famous skeleton dancers, whose striking body paint mimics the dead and represents ancestral spirits.

Visiting a local village offers a profound insight into a way of life that has been preserved for centuries. Engage with local artisans, listen to the stories of the elders, and gain a deeper appreciation for the enduring spirit of the Highlands people.

Plan Your Adventure

Essential tips for your journey to Chimbu Province.

⛄

Best Time to Visit

The dry season, from May to October, is ideal for trekking and outdoor activities, offering clearer skies and more manageable trails.

🛫

Getting There

Fly into Goroka (GKA) or Mount Hagen (HGU) and travel by road to Kundiawa, the provincial capital. Hiring a local guide and vehicle is highly recommended.

🏘

Where to Stay

Accommodations range from guesthouses in Kundiawa to rustic lodges near Mount Wilhelm. Booking in advance is essential, especially during peak season.
